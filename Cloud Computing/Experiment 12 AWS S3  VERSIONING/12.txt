Log in to your AWS account and go to the S3 service.

Click on "Create Bucket" and provide a unique name for your bucket.

Choose the region for your bucket and click on "Create".

Once the bucket is created, click on it and go to the "Properties" tab.

Click on "Versioning" and enable versioning for your bucket.

Now, go to the "Permissions" tab and click on "Bucket Policy".

Provide appropriate permissions for your bucket according to your requirements.

Now, go to the "Overview" tab and click on "Upload".

Choose the file that you want to upload and click on "Upload".

Once the file is uploaded, you can see it in the bucket.

To view the version history of the uploaded file, click on the file and go to the "Versions" tab.

Here, you can see all the versions of the file along with their details.

You can also perform various operations on the uploaded file, such as downloading, deleting, and copying.

Once you are done with the implementation, make sure to terminate the instances or delete the bucket to avoid unnecessary charges.
